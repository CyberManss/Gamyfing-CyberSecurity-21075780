3. Recognizing Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks can deceive you into giving up sensitive information like usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, and other confidential information. The attacker, hides as a trustworthy entity, like a banker or a company, to trick you into opening an email, instant message, or text message. These evil attacks come in forms of emails, text messages, or even social media messages. These messages tend to ask you to open some sort of link or download an attachement. Below, you can see the common types of phising attacks:

  1. EMAIL PHISHING, is the most common phishing. Attackers send you fraudulent emails that look like they are trustworthy and look like they are from a legitimate company email. These emails tend to ask you to click on a link or download an attachment. These are methods are designed to steal your private inforamtion. [Will include example]
  2. SPEAR PHISHING, is a more targetted form of phishing. This means evil attackers, sutdy you and customise a specific message for you or organisation. You will know this is a spear phishing attack because they often use your personal information to make you feel vulnerable. [Will include example]
  3. WHALING, is another form of phishing and used to target high-profile individuals/organisations. [Will include example]
  4. VISHING, this form of phishing attack occurs over the phone. They pretend to be a company worker such as a bank agent. Using their fake identity, they tend to ask for your personal information like your personal details.
